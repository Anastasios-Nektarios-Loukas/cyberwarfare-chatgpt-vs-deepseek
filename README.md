# Generative AI in Cybersecurity: A Study on Cyber Warfare

## Overview
This repository contains the research project **"The Utility of Artificial Intelligence in Cybersecurity Knowledge"**, specifically focusing on the domain of **Cyber Warfare**. The project was developed for the Information Systems Security course (2024-2025) at the Athens University of Economics and Business (AUEB).

The study explores the capabilities and limitations of Generative AI tools in synthesizing academic knowledge and evaluating complex cybersecurity threats.

## Key Features
- **Theoretical Analysis:** Comprehensive definition and categorization of Cyber Warfare, including espionage, sabotage, DDoS, and propaganda.
- **Comparative AI Evaluation:** A differential evaluation between **ChatGPT-4o** and **DeepSeek R1**.
- **Historical Case Studies:** Detailed analysis of:
  - **Stuxnet (2010):** Industrial sabotage of nuclear facilities.
  - **Sony Pictures Hack (2014):** Corporate espionage and state-sponsored attacks.
  - **Ukraine Power Grid Attack (2015-2016):** Infrastructure disruption and cyber-kinetic effects.
- **Prompt Engineering:** Documentation of the prompts used to generate and validate the report content.

## Methodology
The project followed a two-phase approach:
1. **Phase A:** Content generation using ChatGPT-4o to establish a theoretical baseline.
2. **Phase B:** Validation and cross-referencing using DeepSeek R1 and independent academic bibliography to ensure accuracy.
